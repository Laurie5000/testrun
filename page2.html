THE WONDERS OF MADISON SQUARE PARK

SOUTH SIDE

The south side of the park has monuments to William Seward and Roscoe Conkling.

William Seward

There is a seated man at the corner of 23rd Street and Fifth Avenue. This is an excellent monument of William Seward who, among other accomplishments, negotiated the purchase of Alaska for the United States.

Roscoe Conkling

The corner of 23rd Street and Madison Avenue has a statue of Roscoe Conkling. He was a U.S. senator during the late nineteenth century.



Madison Square Park is located at 23rd-26th Streets between Madison and Broadway Avenues. It is easily accessible by 6/N/R/F/M trains.
